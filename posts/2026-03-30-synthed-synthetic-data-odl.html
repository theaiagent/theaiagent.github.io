Building SynthEd: Agent-Based Synthetic Data for ODL Research

March 30, 2026 · 10 min read
Synthetic Data Agent-Based Simulation Python ODL

The Problem

Educational data mining research has a data problem. If you want to study why students drop out of Open and Distance Learning (ODL) programs, you need access to student interaction logs, demographic profiles, engagement trajectories, and outcome records. But privacy regulations like GDPR and KVKK make this data extremely difficult to obtain.

Existing synthetic data approaches — GANs, VAEs, SMOTE — can generate numbers that look statistically similar, but they produce temporally incoherent data. A GAN does not know that a student who failed a midterm should show declining engagement in the following weeks. It just generates rows.

I wanted something different: synthetic data where every interaction trace is behaviorally grounded in a simulated student's personality, motivation, life circumstances, and evolving academic experience.

The Approach: Theory-Driven Agent Simulation

SynthEd takes a fundamentally different approach. Instead of learning patterns from existing data, it simulates students as autonomous agents, each with a rich persona grounded in established educational psychology theories.

Each simulated student has:

  • Big Five personality traits (Costa & McCrae, 1992) that influence study habits and social behavior
  • SDT motivation type (Deci & Ryan, 1985) that evolves during the simulation based on need satisfaction
  • Environmental pressures (Bean & Metzner, 1985) — employment, family responsibilities, financial stress
  • Digital skills and self-regulation (Rovai, 2003) that determine persistence capacity
  • Learner autonomy (Moore, 1993) that interacts with course structure to produce transactional distance

These are not just static attributes. The simulation runs week by week, with each student generating realistic LMS interactions (logins, forum posts, assignment submissions, exam scores) based on their current engagement level, which itself evolves through the interplay of all theoretical frameworks.

Two-Phase Weekly Loop

The simulation uses a two-phase architecture inspired by agent-based social simulation (Epstein & Axtell, 1996):

Phase 1 (Individual): Each student independently generates interactions, updates their academic/social integration (Tinto), Community of Inquiry presences (Garrison), SDT need satisfaction, and exhaustion level (Gonzalez et al., 2025). Engagement is then updated through a multi-theory composite formula.

Phase 2 (Social): Students who posted in the same forum form peer connections. The social network mediates engagement contagion — connected students pull each other's engagement toward the local mean. When a student enters advanced dropout phases, their neighbors face increased dropout risk (dropout cascades).

Dropout itself follows a 6-phase process model based on Bäulke, Grunschel & Dresel (2022): non-fit perception → thoughts of quitting → deliberation → information search → final decision, with recovery possible at every stage.

Multi-Semester Simulation

Real students do not experience 56 continuous weeks of pressure. Between semesters there are breaks, renewed motivation, and fresh starts. SynthEd models this with inter-semester carry-over mechanics:

  • Engagement partially recovers during the break
  • Social network links decay (new cohort effect)
  • Dropout phase regresses by one level (fresh start)
  • Academic exhaustion partially recovers
  • Academic integration and knowledge carry over fully

With these mechanics, a 4-semester simulation produces ~95% cumulative dropout (200 → 104 → 50 → 16 → 10 surviving), which aligns with ODL literature reporting 40–80% dropout rates per cohort.

17 Statistical Validation Tests

How do you know synthetic data is any good? SynthEd includes a built-in validation suite that automatically checks 17 statistical tests across all theoretical frameworks:

  • KS-tests and chi-squared tests for demographic distributions
  • Correlation checks: conscientiousness ↔ dropout, self-efficacy ↔ engagement, financial stress ↔ dropout
  • Temporal coherence: dropout students must show declining engagement trajectories
  • SDT validation: intrinsically motivated students should have higher engagement than amotivated ones
  • Privacy: k-anonymity check on quasi-identifiers

The pipeline currently achieves A (Excellent) quality with 17/17 tests passing on the default configuration.

What Is Next

SynthEd is under active development. The next major milestone is OULAD-compatible export — generating data in the exact format of the Open University Learning Analytics Dataset so researchers can use SynthEd as a drop-in replacement or augmentation for their existing EDM workflows.

The full source code, documentation, and benchmark profiles are available on GitHub, and the project is archived with a DOI on Zenodo.
